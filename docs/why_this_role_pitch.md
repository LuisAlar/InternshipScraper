# The "Why This Role?" Pitch: Empathy & Human-Facing Solutions

When Jordan or the Executive Sponsor asks, *"Why do you want this position?"* or *"What interests you about this role?"*, they expect a generic answer about "loving AI." 

Instead, use this script to deliver a profound, mature answer that positions you as a deeply empathetic problem solver.

---

## 1. The Core Philosophy (The Hook)
Start by stating your fundamental belief about software and AI. 

> *"My main interest in this role is that it’s fundamentally about solving problems for humans, not just writing code. I'm drawn to solutions that are tightly coupled with human needs and empathy. Many people look at AI as a way to replace human dynamics, but I view my job as building workflows that actually elevate the human experience."*

## 2. The Evidence (Connecting Your Past Experience)
Directly map this philosophy to your resume to prove this isn't just talk.

> *"If you look at my background, my most successful projects have always lived at the intersection of complex systems and human friction:*
> *   *At **Orbital Mentorship**, my focus wasn't just building DevOps pipelines; it was looking at the human pain point of developer onboarding and reducing that friction by 50%.*
> *   *At **Indigo Magazine**, my role as Web Development Lead forced me to build technical workflows that didn't alienate our creative writers and designers, but actually empowered them.*
> *   *As a **Solutions Engineer at Xtensible**, my entire job was bridging the gap between software developers and IT operations. I had to understand the pain points of both sides to automate their infrastructure effectively."*

## 3. The Action Plan (Your First 30 Days)
This is the most critical part of the pitch. Tell them exactly what you will do on day one. It proves you understand the assignment.

> *"Because of this human-centric approach, I know that if I am brought on for this internship, the very first thing I'd do wouldn't be opening a low-code canvas. My first step would be sitting down to have conversations with your sales reps.*
> 
> *I want to learn their actual workflows. I want to know what they think of AI, where they believe it could improve their day-to-day, and, just as importantly, the ways they absolutely **do not** want to use it. You can't build a high-ROI workflow without first understanding the empathy and opinions of the people being forced to use it. That human discovery phase is what I excel at, and that’s why I want this role."*

---

## How to Practice This
1.  **Don't memorize it word-for-word.** Memorize the 3 main beats: **(1) Empathy/Human focus, (2) My past experience proves this, (3) I will interview the sales reps first.**
2.  **Pacing:** When delivering this, slow down. When you say "the ways they absolutely *do not* want to use it," pause for effect. It shows immense maturity to acknowledge that users might reject a technology if built poorly.
